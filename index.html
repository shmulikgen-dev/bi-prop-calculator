📚 Equations & Theory

1. Chemical Induction (\(\tau_{id}\))

$$ \tau_{id} = A \cdot \frac{1}{C^m \cdot P_c^k} \cdot e^{\frac{E_a}{R \cdot T}} $$

The delay from initial liquid contact until reaction onset. Depends inversely on additive concentration (C) and chamber pressure (Pc). Rising temperature decreases the delay exponentially via the activation energy (Ea).

2. Vaporization Delay (\(\tau_{vap}\))

$$ \tau_{vap} = \frac{SMD^2}{K_{evap} \cdot P_c^q} $$

Based on the \(D^2\)-law of droplet evaporation. The time required for atomized droplets (Sauter Mean Diameter) to vaporize prior to combustion. Finer droplets quadratically reduce this time.

3. Total Time Lag (\(\tau\))

$$ \tau = \tau_{id} + \tau_{vap} $$

Crocco's time lag model. The total interval from propellant injection into the chamber until bulk heat release.

4. Acoustic Resonance (\(1T\))

$$ f_{1T} = \frac{1.841 \cdot c}{\pi \cdot D} $$

The First Tangential acoustic resonance mode. Destructive instability (Screaming) occurs when the time lag (\(\tau\)) synchronizes with the acoustic wave period: \(\tau \approx \frac{1}{2 \cdot f_{1T}}\).

      📚 Engine Sizing & Cooling Equations

      1. Mass Flow (\(\dot{m}\))

      $$ \dot{m} = \frac{F}{I_{sp} \cdot g_0} $$

      The total mass flow rate required to achieve the target Thrust (\(F\)). Derived from the specific impulse (\(I_{sp}\)). It is split into oxidizer and fuel based on the O/F ratio.

      2. Throat Area (\(A_t\))

      $$ A_t = \frac{\dot{m} \cdot c^*}{P_c} $$

      The most critical dimension of the engine (choke point). Depends on the mass flow, chamber pressure (\(P_c\)), and characteristic velocity (\(c^*\)) of the propellants.

      3. Chamber Dimensions (\(L_c\), \(A_c\))

      $$ A_c = CR \cdot A_t \quad | \quad L_c = \frac{L^*}{CR} $$

      The chamber area is scaled from the throat area using the Contraction Ratio (\(CR\)). The physical length (\(L_c\)) is derived from the Characteristic Length (\(L^*\)), assuming a cylindrical volume.

      4. Wall Thickness (\(t_w\))

      $$ t_w = \frac{P_c \cdot D_c}{2 \cdot \sigma_{allow}} $$

      Thin-wall pressure vessel hoop stress. Determines the thickness of the inner hot-wall based on the material's allowable stress (\(\sigma_{allow}\)) and the internal pressure.

      5. Cooling Channels (\(N\), \(w_t\))

      $$ N = \lfloor \frac{\pi \cdot D_t}{w_{min} + t_{rib}} \rfloor $$

      Calculates the maximum number of channels (\(N\)) that can physically fit around the throat perimeter while maintaining a minimum channel width (\(w_{min} = 1.5mm\)) and the specified rib thickness.

      5b. Rib Tensile Stress (\(t_{rib\_min}\))

      $$ t_{rib\_min} = \frac{\Delta P \cdot p_c}{\sigma_{allow}} $$

      Structural integrity check. The minimum rib thickness required to withstand the tensile force exerted by the coolant pressure differential (\(\Delta P\)) on the channel pitch (\(p_c\)). The rib thickness is automatically increased if this structural limit exceeds the manufacturing minimum.

      6. Hot Gas Heat Transfer (Bartz)

      $$ h_g \propto \frac{1}{D_t^{0.2}} \cdot \left(\frac{P_c}{c^*}\right)^{0.8} $$

      The Bartz equation estimates the convective heat transfer coefficient (\(h_g\)) of the hot combustion gas. It is highest at the throat due to extreme velocity and mass flux.

      7. Coolant Heat Transfer (Dittus-Boelter)

      $$ Nu = 0.023 \cdot Re^{0.8} \cdot Pr^{0.4} $$

      Calculates the convective heat transfer coefficient (\(h_c\)) of the liquid coolant inside the channels based on the flow velocity (Reynolds number).

      8. Steady-State Heat Flux (\(q\))

      $$ q = \frac{T_{c} - T_{co}}{\frac{1}{h_g} + \frac{t_w}{k_w} + \frac{1}{h_c}} $$

      The 1D thermal resistance network. Balances the heat flowing from the gas (\(T_c\)), through the metal wall (\(k_w/t_w\)), and into the liquid coolant (\(T_{co}\)).

      9. Engine Mass Optimization (\(M_{tot}\))

      $$ M_{tot} = M_{liner} + M_{jacket} + M_{ribs} $$

      Calculates structural mass of the combustion chamber, nozzle, jacket, and ribs based on geometry and density (\(\rho_{mat}\)). Excludes the injector, flanges, and valves. Used to find the optimal Contraction Ratio (CR) that minimizes total mass.
